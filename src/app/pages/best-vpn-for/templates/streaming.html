Streaming 101

Streaming is often confused with downloading, as they both essentially do the same thing – allow you to enjoy the content you love. Where they differ is in the way they deliver the content to your device. Streaming is when you consume media that’s playing in real-time. This means that the media is not saved on your device and you need internet access the entire time that the content is playing. Streaming speed depends on internet connection strength, which is why streaming is choppy at times. Downloading, on the other hand, is when the media file is transferred entirely to your computer, giving you access whenever you want, even without an internet connection.

Streaming has risen in popularity due to the fact that it’s a safer way to consume content, as nothing is being stored on your computer, and it removes a lot of the hassle that comes with downloading. The media may be stored in a cloud or accessed by a media streamer. Either way, the file is available and requires no download or link-copying on your part.

You can stream content anywhere, anytime, and on any device. No matter if you’re commuting to work, relaxing in the park, or running on the treadmill, streaming is generally quick, safe, and reliable.

Using a VPN to Stream

They’re really simple to set up. All you have to do is:

  1. Turn on your TV
  2. Open the streaming feature
  3. Select the show or movie you want to watch
  4. Sit back, relax, and enjoy the show (literally).

There are also online streaming websites like Netflix, YouTube, and Kodi that stream movies, series, and music directly to your phone or computer

Common uses for streaming are of course to watch movies and series, but other content can be streamed like news programs and sporting events. After pressing play, the content gets streamed directly to your device in real-time. Keep in mind that you will need to have good internet connection so the content plays smoothly. This is especially true for high quality videos.
