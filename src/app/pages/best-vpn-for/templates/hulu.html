How to Choose a Hulu VPN

With so much quality content riding on a subscription, it’s not surprising that Hulu has such a wide fan base. There’s one problem though—geofilters. A geofilter blocks content streaming when you are outside the geographic parameters defined by the service. In the case of Hulu, anyone outside the US or Japan won’t be able to access their favorite television shows, even if you have an account.

Fortunately, there’s an easy way around all of these restrictions. VPNs are services that set up a virtual private network. How to Disable IPv6

Because even the best VPNs for Hulu won't hide your IPv6 (only your IPv4 addresses), you'll need to disable these in order for your virtual private network workaround to be successful. The good news is that this is super simple. Here's how to disable IPv6 for Windows 10 (this should be done before connecting to the VPN. If you're already connected, disconnect, close your app, and then proceed):

  1. Activate the Run prompt by holding down Win Key+R
  2. Open Network Connections by typing in ncpa.cpl and press enter
  3. Right click on the connection you're using to connect to the internet and find the Properties option
  4. Find the option for Internet Protocol Version 6 (TCP/IPv6) and uncheck the box

Don't forget to save changes. Next, you have to update these changes:

  1. Type Win Key+R to open the Command Prompt. You’ll be asked if it’s all right for the program to make changes, and you should click yes.
  2. A black screen will appear. Type ipconfig/flushdns, and press enter.
  3. You’re done.

Now, you can reconnect to your VPN, connect to Hulu, and stream away.

If you are running MacOS, then follow these instructions to disable IPv6:

  1. Disconnect and close your VPN as before.
  2. Open your System Preferences in the menu bar, select Network, AirPort, and Advanced in that order.
  3. Select TCP/IP and Configure IPv6. When the menu pops up, choose Off.
  4. Apply changes, and reconnect to your VPN.
