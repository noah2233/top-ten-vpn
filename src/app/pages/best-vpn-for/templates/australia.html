Whether you live in Australia or are just visiting, the best thing you can do to protect your online privacy and access content from around the world is to use a VPN, or virtual private network.

Why do you need a VPN for Australia?

Few Australians realize it, but the authorities have the power to instruct your internet service provider (ISP) to hand over certain information about internet users, such as the IP address the user was connected to at the time of sending or receiving communications. In 2015-16, the various law enforcement agencies made more than 300,000 authorizations for the disclosure of historical telecommunications data, including IP addresses. Another benefit of using a VPN for Australia is that it can get you around geo-blocking restrictions. Many streaming services, such as Netflix, Amazon, and Hulu restrict certain content to Australian viewers. With a VPN, you can connect to a server in the US, UK or elsewhere while being physically in Australia, and these services will treat you as if you were viewing from LA, London or Delhi and not from Sydney, Melbourne, or Alice.
